About me:

Postdoctoral Fellow in the Moser Group at the Kavli Institute for Systems Neuroscience.
Currently investigating the ontogeny of our sense of space, with a focus on the developmental timeline of toroidal and ring manifolds.
Previously, I developed new implant systems for mice and studied temporal codes in CA1 1;2.

On the side: poetry, history, mushrooms and the mediterranean sea.

About my research:

My broader scientific interest centers on understanding how biological systems learn to coordinate sensation and movement to generate flexible and adaptive behaviors during early development. I approach this challenge by combining two complementary perspectives:
circuit-level connectivity and population dynamics analyses.
